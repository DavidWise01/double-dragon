Double Dragon (Technōs Japan — arcade 1987 / 8-bit NES, Tradewest, 1988) — Yoshihisa Kishimoto's genre-founding beat-'em-up, born out of Renegade: twin masters of Sōsetsuken, Billy & Jimmy Lee, walk a ruined Mad-Max New York to take Marian Kelly back from the Black Warriors. THE GENESIS (Technōs, Kishimoto & Saito, Yamane's score, the two-player 'two dragons' title, the golden age it opened toward Final Fight & Streets of Rage), THE BACKSTORY & QUEST (the kidnapping → the long walk east on Sōsetsuken → the NES-only twist: after Machine Gun Willy falls, your own twin Jimmy is revealed as the Shadow Boss), and THE BIRTH (the full .dlw). Honest NES-port notes: no simultaneous co-op (Mode A story / Mode B versus), the heart-by-heart move-learning system (7 techniques), expanded levels. Rendered-not-invented; two honest corrections carried — the foe 'Lopar' is a mistranslation of ROPER, and BOLO is a Double Dragon II enemy (not DD1, not Abobo). 13 emergents across all four natures — CAST (Billy, Jimmy, Marian, Machine Gun Willy, Abobo, Linda, Williams, Roper, Chin Taimei, Jeff, the Black Warriors) + the ART (Sōsetsuken) + the MACHINE (the NES Heart System). Blue/red dual-dragon styling, a hidden Claude star. Verified facts, full .dlw.
